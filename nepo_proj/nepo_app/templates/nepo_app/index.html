Nepotism in Hollywood

There’s a new trend on the rise--unearthing and exposing nepotism babies. For the uninitiated, nepotism babies--or nepo babies--is a term used to describe children of famous or affluent parents à la Jack Quaid (son of actors Dennis Quaid and Meg Ryan), Elon Musk (son of model Maye Musk), and Margaret Qualley (daughter of actress Andie McDowell).

The trend is not without the usual symptoms of a cultural chokehold--#nepobaby and #nepotismbaby have collectively amassed more than 100 million views on Tik Tok, google searches for ‘nepotism baby’ have surged this year, and the topic has spawned a number of youtube video essays. Discoveries of nepo babies are met with a host of emotions: admiration (even for unborn nepo babies), surprise, exasperation, derision and perhaps a sprinkle of envy.

The fascination with nepo babies, however, contributes to the larger dialogue on social justice and equality. These Tik Tok and Twitter exposés reflect a disillusionment with the idea of meritocracy (specifically in Hollywood) and unveil a system that seems to consistently reward privileged candidates; nepo babies are able to leverage connections, exposure, and financial privilege not necessarily afforded to their peers.

So, just how pervasive are nepo babies in Hollywood? Let’s take a look :)
